Written Information and Informed Consent Form The study "Artifical syneshesia. Can color support interval recognition learning process?"

Objective

Ability to recognize musical intervals (distance between two notes) is a useful skill which might be trained. Some people are able to connect music sounds with colors or others senses. This connection might concerns single notes, chords, key etc. This research is trying to check will music interval supported by displayed color or picture be easier to remeber. This study is conducted by a Master student at NTNU in Gj√∏vik and is a part of one of the mandatory courses - IMT4315 Color in Interface Design (spring 2018).

Procedure

During the test will be presented four short learning parts where you will be introduced to a basic intervals. The intervals consist of two notes. All intervals starts from the same note and differ from each other by second note. In some learning parts intervals are connected to the colors or pictures. You are encouraged to use that knowledge. After each learning part there will be short test consisted from 8 questions where you will need to recognize interval. After finishing test you might be ask to send back received JSON file.

Duration

10-15 minutes

Confidentality

Your score will be save into JSON file. Participants will be distinguish by date-time stamp and they will be anonimous.
