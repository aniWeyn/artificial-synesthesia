Written Information and Informed Consent Form The study "Artifical syneshesia. Can color support interval recognition learning process?"

Objective

The ability to recognize musical intervals (distance between two notes) is a useful skill which might be trained. Some people are able to connect music sounds with colours or others senses. This connection might concerns single notes, chords, key etc. This research is trying to check will music interval be easier to remember if supported by colours or pictures. This study is conducted by a Master student at NTNU in Gj√∏vik and is a part of one of the mandatory courses - IMT4315 Color in Interface Design (spring 2018).

Procedure

During the test, you will be presented four short learning phases where you will be introduced to the basic intervals. Each interval is made up of two notes. All intervals will start on the same note and differ from each other by the second one. In some learning phases, the intervals will be paired with colors or pictures. You are encouraged to remember this connection and try to use it during the task. After each learning phase, you will be asked to take a short test, eight questions asking you to recognize a series of intervals. After finishing the test you might be asked to send back received JSON file.
